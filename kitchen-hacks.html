Vegetable broth

When making soup or stew, instead of throwing away vegetable scraps like carrot tops, onion skins, and celery ends, save them in a freezer bag. Once the bag is full, use the scraps to make homemade vegetable broth. It's a great way to reduce food waste and add extra flavor to your dishes without spending extra money on store-bought broth.

Citrus flavor cubes

Use Citrus Zest: Before juicing a lemon, lime, or orange, grate the zest (the outer colored part of the peel) first. Freeze the zest in small portions in an ice cube tray. It's a quick way to add bright citrus flavor to dishes without having to zest a fresh fruit every time.

Homemade Croutons

Don't throw away stale bread. Cube it, toss it with olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and any other desired seasonings, then bake in the oven until golden brown. Homemade croutons are perfect for salads or soups.
